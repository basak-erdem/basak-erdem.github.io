Critical Thinking \& Problem Solving

Essential Employability Skill – Artifact & Reflection

Artifact — Policy Analysis Report

📄 Download Report (Word)

Description: A policy analysis report titled “Workplace Violence and Harassment: A Policy Analysis,” completed for SOCI 1204 – Office Professionalism II at Durham College (March 2026, Professor Kelly Webdale). The report compares how Durham College and Walmart handle workplace violence and harassment, examines Ontario’s Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA), and proposes recommendations for improvement.

Reflection

Self-Assessment: Exemplary

EES Outcome & Artifact

This policy analysis demonstrates my ability to apply critical thinking and problem-solving skills to a real-world workplace issue. I selected this artifact because it required me to research legislation, compare organizational policies, evaluate their effectiveness, and propose evidence-based recommendations — all essential components of critical thinking.

Successes & Challenges

  • Researched and analyzed Ontario’s Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA) to understand the legal framework governing workplace violence and harassment policies
  • Conducted a comparative analysis of two very different organizations — Durham College (educational institution) and Walmart (retail corporation) — evaluating the strengths and gaps in each organization’s approach
  • Identified a critical gap: having a policy on paper is not the same as effectively protecting employees, and many incidents go unreported because workers do not know what to do or who to contact
  • Developed actionable recommendations for what both organizations could do better, demonstrating the ability to move beyond analysis to propose practical solutions

New Learning & Impact on Future

  • Gained a deeper understanding of how workplace safety legislation translates into organizational policy, and the importance of clear, accessible communication so all employees understand their rights and reporting procedures
  • Strengthened my research and analytical writing skills by synthesizing legal requirements, real-world policies, and personal evaluation into a cohesive, structured report
  • These critical thinking skills are essential in medical office administration where I will need to interpret healthcare regulations, evaluate office procedures, resolve patient and staff concerns, and recommend process improvements to support a safe and efficient clinic environment
