Communication Skills

Essential Employability Skill – Artifact & Reflection

Artifact — Business Memorandum

📄 View Full Memo (PDF)

Description: A professional interoffice memorandum prepared for Lavender Farms regarding upcoming marketing campaigns. The memo demonstrates proper business formatting including company letterhead, To/Cc/From fields, subject line, clear body paragraphs with supporting data (11.3% cost increase, 20-40% revenue projections), campaign timelines, and reference initials.

Reflection

EES Outcome & Artifact

This memorandum demonstrates my ability to communicate clearly, concisely, and correctly in written form. I selected this artifact because it showcases professional business correspondence with proper formatting, audience-appropriate tone, and structured content delivery.

Self-Assessment

I would assess my communication skills at the exemplary level. The memo follows all professional formatting conventions, uses audience-appropriate tone, incorporates supporting data to strengthen its message, and includes a clear call to action — reflecting the confidence and precision I have developed through 17 years of communicating with students, parents, colleagues, and clients across diverse cultural settings.

Successes & Challenges

  • Applied standard memo formatting including To, Cc, From, Date, Subject fields, and reference initials — following professional business conventions
  • Structured the message with a clear problem statement, proposed solution, supporting data (11.3% cost increase, 20-30% revenue projections), and a specific call to action
  • Adapted tone for a professional audience, balancing urgency ("It is critical that we get this plan right") with collaborative language ("We ask each of you to review")
  • Drawing on 17 years of experience communicating with students, parents, and colleagues across diverse cultural backgrounds strengthened my ability to write for different audiences
  • Challenge: One difficulty was striking the right balance between urgency and professionalism — the memo needed to convey that immediate action was required without sounding alarming or pressuring. I overcame this by revising the tone multiple times, using collaborative language like "We ask each of you to review" alongside factual data to let the numbers speak for themselves

New Learning & Impact on Future

  • Learned the importance of including specific dates, data points, and clear action items to make business correspondence effective and actionable
  • Developed stronger awareness of how document formatting and structure contribute to the credibility and professionalism of written communication
  • These skills directly prepare me for medical office environments where clear written communication with physicians, patients, insurance companies, and interdepartmental teams is essential
